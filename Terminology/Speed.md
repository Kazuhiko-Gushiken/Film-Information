# Speed

In film photography, "speed" can mean different things in different contexts:

In terms of film itself:
- Speed refers to the ISO. A higher speed ISO (e.g. 1600, 3200, 6400+) increases the camera's sensitivity to light. A lower speed ISO does the opposite.

In terms of aperture:
- A higher speed aperture lens (often called a "fast" lens) is a camera lens with a very wide maximum aperture (e.g. f/1.2).

In terms of shutter speed:
- A faster shutter speed reduces the time the film is exposed for, not only reducing the light that hits the film, but also reducing the amount of time a subject is captured for (e.g. 1/1000s freezing a speeding car).
